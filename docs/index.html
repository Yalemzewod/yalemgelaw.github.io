Welcome !

I am a research scientist with a PhD in Epidemiology from the University of Queensland in Australia. My wider research focuses on preventing and controlling infectious diseases, such as tuberculosis, malaria, and HIV, using geostatistical models. I have published a number of articles in peer-review journals, and I also presented my research at various national and international conferences.

Currently, I work as a research officer at the Telethon Kids Institute of Malaria Atlas Project (MAP), where I assist with data management, analysis, modelling and research in malaria epidemiology and prevention and elimination programs. With over ten years of teaching and research experience in Australia and Ethiopia, I have gained valuable insights into the challenges of preventing and controlling infectious diseases in low-resource settings.
